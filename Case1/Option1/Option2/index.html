Alright you and the authorities have access to Ronald’s computer. They run a program that scans Ronald’s past searches in Internet Explorer. There could be a chance that he looked up topics related to monkeys during his preparation for the crime. This is it - the moment of truth. They scan it ... and no words related to monkey stealing come up. What should you do?

Your Options

  • Give up - the evidence speaks for itself and there is nothing you can do.
  • Present to the court that because there are no searches, Ronald must have deleted his search history and be guilty.
  • Run a program that looks into the search history of other browsers on Ronald’s computer.
