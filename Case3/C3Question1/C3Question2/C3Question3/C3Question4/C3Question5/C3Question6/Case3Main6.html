For his next challenge Mr. Evil Noodle gives you the case of Ms. Asparagus. Ms. Asparagus was charged for scamming money online from others. A piece of evidence that was presented to the court was a conversation between two people talking over text about how they heard about Ms. Asparagus scamming others. However, Ms. Asparagus claimed innocence and appealed on the basis that the evidence of the conversation would fall under hearsay and therefore should not have been presented in court. Is this true? (You can click the question mark below if you would like more details about hearsay)

Your Options

  • Yes - testimony about another person’s word often falls under hearsay because it makes an assertion that may not reflect what truly happened.

  • No - while this would be considered hearsay, hearsay is often still allowed to be used in court

  • Yes - testimony about another person’s word on text falls under hearsay because it is making an assertion about another on a digital medium.

  • No - testimony about another person’s word does not fall hearsay.
